# Smoked Brisket

## Context

- I live near Phoenix, Arizona, so the air is dry; therefore, use a water pan.
- Aim to finish the actual "cooking" to allow for a **long, 4 to 6-hour rested hold**. 

## Focus here is a "Tenderness-First" schedule:

---

### The Saturday 5:00 PM Timeline

| Phase | Time | Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Start Smoker** | **Tonight (Fri) 9:00 PM** | Aim for a steady **225°F**. |
| **Meat On** | **Tonight (Fri) 10:00 PM** | Place brisket on cold. Add a water pan to the smoker. |
| **The Wrap** | **Sat 6:00 AM – 7:00 AM** | Brisket should be ~$165°F. Wrap tightly in **foil**. |
| **The Finish** | **Sat 10:00 AM – 11:00 AM** | Pull when it hits **203°F** and feels like soft butter. |

| **The Hold** | **Sat 11:30 AM – 4:30 PM** | Re-wrap and place in a cooler or 150°F oven. |
| **Slice & Serve**| **Sat 5:00 PM** | Slice against the grain and enjoy. |

### Why this specific plan for Tenderness?

**1. Foil vs. Paper (The "Texas Crutch")**
Since you mentioned **bark is secondary**, use **heavy-duty aluminum foil** instead of butcher paper. Foil traps 100% of the moisture and steam. This effectively "braises" the brisket during the final few hours of cooking, which is the fastest way to break down the stubborn connective tissue and guarantee tenderness.

**2. The "Faux Cambro" Hold**
A brisket pulled at 11:00 AM and served at 5:00 PM is actually *better* than one served immediately.
* Take your wrapped brisket, wrap it in **two large bath towels**, and put it into a **dry plastic cooler**. 
* Fill any empty space in the cooler with more towels. 
* The brisket will stay piping hot (well above 145°F) for those 6 hours. During this time, the muscle fibers relax and reabsorb the rendered fats, making it incredibly succulent.

**3. The Water Pan**
In the dry Arizona air, the surface of the meat can dry out and become "leathery" before the inside is tender. Keep a large pan of water inside the smoker right next to the heat source. This humidity creates a "sauna" effect that keeps the meat supple.

### Critical Safety Check
When you pull it off the smoker at ~11:00 AM, don't just throw it straight into the cooler. Let it sit on the counter for about **20–30 minutes** first. You want the internal temperature to drop to around **180°F** before you tuck it away. If you put it in the cooler while it's still actively "climbing" in temp, it could overcook into "pot roast" territory and fall apart when you try to slice it.
